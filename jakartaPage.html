The Legacy of Diplomacy

Background and Rationale

Following successful editions of the Young Public Diplomacy Summit (YPDS) in Baku, Istanbul, and Tashkent, the summit continues to expand as a platform for engaging young leaders in international dialogue and diplomacy.

Jakarta, as the capital of Indonesia, serves as a key center of diplomacy, governance, and international cooperation, hosting numerous embassies, policy institutions, and academic centers. Its strategic position at the heart of Southeast Asia makes it an ideal setting for discussions on public diplomacy and global engagement.

Target Participants

Emerging youth leaders, university students, and young professionals (Ages 16–40)

Representing regions from Asia, Europe, Africa, and the Middle East.

The Destination

The Jakarta Experience

More than just a forum—an immersion into the heart of Indonesia.

Delegates will experience Jakarta not just as tourists, but as active participants in its enduring legacy of statecraft. The forum includes exclusive access to historic sites, guided architectural walks discussing modern governance, and networking events set against the backdrop of iconic Indonesian landmarks.

  • Exclusive venue hosting inside a prominent policy institution.
  • Curated cultural excursions including historic Jakarta and its iconic landmarks.
  • Authentic Indonesian culinary experiences and networking dinners.

The Young Public Diplomacy Summit (YPDS) is an international platform that brings together emerging youth leaders, policymakers, academics, and professionals to engage in meaningful dialogue on diplomacy, global governance, and international cooperation.

Following successful editions in Baku, Istanbul, and Tashkent, the summit continues its global expansion in Jakarta—one of the most significant diplomatic and political hubs in Southeast Asia.

Jakarta, the capital of Indonesia, is a center of diplomacy, governance, and international collaboration. It hosts numerous embassies, policy institutions, and academic centers, making it an ideal environment for discussions on public diplomacy and global engagement.

The summit is open to:

  • Emerging youth leaders
  • University students
  • Young professionals

Participants are generally responsible for their own travel, accommodation, and related expenses. To support outstanding applicants and promote accessibility, the Forum offers the following scholarship opportunities:

Fully Funded Scholarships (10 Awards)

Recipients will receive:

  • Accommodation during the Forum
  • Reception dinner and two international meals per day
  • Full Forum access and participation
  • Local transportation
  • Certificate of participation
  • Airfare support ranging from USD 300 to USD 1,000, based on the participant's country of residence and actual travel costs
  • If the cost of an economy-class ticket is below USD 300, the full ticket cost will be covered

Outstanding participants may also be invited to volunteer or collaborate with the Center for Strategy and Cultural Diplomacy (CSCD) following the Forum. Additional support may be considered based on the participant's performance during the Forum, commitment to the program, and subsequent contributions to CSCD initiatives.

Partially Funded Scholarships (20 Awards)

Recipients will receive:

  • A 50% reduction in the self-financed participation fee

Additional Financial Support

Further scholarships and financial assistance may be available on a case-by-case basis. Awards are determined according to:

  • Academic and professional merit
  • Financial need
  • Leadership potential
  • Overall application strength
  • Review and approval by the Scholarship Committee
